01 · Diagnosis

The product has the right parts, but not yet one system.

SignalDesk already contains public routes, Payload drafts, advertising primitives, corrections, search, newsletters, city editions and a lazy Three.js layer. Hunt’s Pointe already stages signed drafts. The redesign should connect those assets into a distinctive regional habit instead of replacing the stack.

Revenue = Traffic × RPM
Traffic = Sessions × Pages/Session
RPM = AdDensity × Fill × Viewability × eCPM × (1 − Discrepancy)

The selected business posture is advertising-led, but increasing raw ad count first would optimize the wrong local metric. The single term this engagement drives is viewability: attention-aware positions, reserved geometry, lazy requests and editorial pauses that let ads enter the viewport without displacing or disguising the story.

Testable hypothesis:

Moving from feed-position-only ads to attention-aware, reserved placements should raise viewability by 8–12 percentage points and, if fill and eCPM remain stable, lift RPM roughly 6–9%. This is a hypothesis, not a forecast. Production data must validate it.

02 · The call

Build a regional story world around useful attention.

Yield & Ad Ops

Favors reserved, lazy, visible inventory: one premium leader, natural in-feed units, an article rail and a capped mobile anchor. It rejects refresh until a trusted viewability baseline exists.

Traffic & SEO

Pushes back on density. Server-rendered story value must lead; LCP, INP, CLS, internal linking, author trust and question-shaped passages protect qualified sessions and pages per session.

Editorial Integrity

Requires visible bylines, sources, corrections and hard separation between newsroom, partner studio and standard advertising. AI assists structure; humans choose and publish coverage.

VFX + Game Design

Uses motion to reveal place, hierarchy and state. The “core loop” is scan → understand → choose a city/topic → read → continue. Visual effects must clarify that loop within a low-end mobile budget.

Product decisions

1
Retain Hampton Roads equity.

The mockup uses “Hampton Roads” as the master wordmark, moves History into a section and uses “America begins at the water” as a campaign line. Final legal naming remains an approval gate.

2
Make civic utility the daily habit.

Today, Business, Civic, Culture, Defense & Port, History, Cities and Watch cover the expanded brief without becoming a generic national feed.

3
Separate atmosphere from evidence.

Cinematic illustration carries the brand hero and evergreen packages. News photography, maps and charts document factual stories with captions, credits and alt text.

4
Use Payload as the control plane.

Editorial lifecycle, placement rules, campaigns, blocked categories, sponsorship conflicts, consent policy, roles and audit history live in one owned system.

5
Keep the draft-only boundary.

Hunt’s Pointe may stage and observe status. It cannot publish, bypass review or select coverage because of estimated CPM.

The system is nonpartisan and place-specific. It borrows the confidence of American civic graphics, the tactility of regional print and the clarity of modern digital briefing products without copying any competitor’s visual identity.

Motion and VFX budget

  • Static AVIF/WebP poster is the LCP element. Three.js loads after LCP, on idle, only when motion is allowed and WebGL capability passes.
  • Use the existing client-only dynamic-import pattern in OceanBackground.tsx; extend it into a small scene system rather than adding another renderer.

  • No scroll-jacking, camera fly-through, autoplay sound, full-page shader or decorative WebGL on article pages.

05 · Viewability-led advertising system

Higher density, negotiated against trust and speed.

The selected ad-led posture is implemented as more eligible opportunities, not unconditional requests. Payload defines positions and policy; the ad-decision service chooses guaranteed/direct → PMP → programmatic backfill; the client requests only near-viewable inventory after consent rules are known.

Density rules

  • Under 600 words: no inline unit; only eligible anchor or page-edge sponsorship.
  • 600–1,399 words: one inline unit plus eligible desktop rail.
  • 1,400+ words: second inline opportunity only in the revenue experiment and only after at least 450 words since the prior ad.
  • Subscriber/ad-light entitlement, consent state, device, current viewability and sponsorship conflict can reduce density. They never increase it above the template ceiling.
  • Creative dimensions are known before request; collapse only before first paint when no demand is eligible. Never collapse a requested slot.
  • Do not add timed refresh in launch. A future test requires ≥30 seconds continuous viewability, a per-session cap, a new impression ID and explicit demand-partner permission.

Measurement contract

Events share publication_id, content_id, placement_id, opportunity_id, decision_id, creative_id, campaign_id, anonymous session_id, consent state, experiment assignment, device class, route type and Core Web Vitals context.

EventDecision it supports
ad_opportunityWas the placement eligible, withheld by policy, or never reached?
ad_decisionWhich tier won, at what price, latency and reason?
ad_renderDid the chosen creative actually render without error?
ad_viewableDid it meet the agreed MRC threshold and how long did it remain visible?
ad_clickWas the click attached to a valid issued token and decision?
page_engagementHow did density affect scroll depth, pages/session and return behavior?
06 · Hunt’s Pointe → SignalDesk

Make the safe boundary feel fast.

The existing HMAC seam is the correct architecture. The redesign adds preflight, idempotency, provenance, taxonomy review, media handling and a visible receipt while preserving the server-enforced draft invariant.

Proposed state model

local_draft → preflight_ready → staging → staged → in_review → changes_requested → approved → scheduled → published
Only SignalDesk/Payload roles may cross approved → scheduled/published.

Versioned request contract

POST /api/integrations/hunts-pointe/v1
Idempotency-Key: hp:{document_id}:{source_version}
X-Webhook-Signature: HMAC-SHA256(raw_body)

Required corrections to the current seam

  • Store tags instead of merely accepting them. Preserve all authors or place unmatched names in a review field.
  • Do not auto-create production taxonomy silently. Map known slugs; put unknown values into “suggested taxonomy” for a managing editor.
  • Use an idempotency record so retries return the same draft rather than create duplicates.
  • Copy approved remote media into hr_media server-side; quarantine rights-unknown media.
  • Write an audit event for received, rejected, retried, reviewed and published states.
  • Return a safe admin path and expose read-only downstream status to Hunt’s Pointe.
  • Scope the service identity to create draft articles/media only. It cannot publish, change campaigns or access reader data.

08 · Engineering build plan

Six independently shippable epics.

Epic 0 · Gate

Governance foundation

Add Payload roles/access, advertiser/campaign/placement/audit/integration collections, creative scan states, consent enforcement, retention jobs, ads.txt and sellers.json. Do not enable the revenue-density variant before this epic passes.

Primary files: payload.config.ts, new collections/*, app/api/ads/*, new app/ads.txt/route.ts, app/sellers.json/route.ts, Supabase migrations and RLS tests.

Epic 1 · System

Tokens and shared components

Translate the prototype tokens into app/globals.css; build accessible masthead, section navigation, story cards, smart brief, trust modules and AdFrame. Add Storybook or an internal component route only if the team already uses one; otherwise use Playwright fixture pages.

Primary files: app/globals.css, components/GlobalNav.tsx, components/ArticleCard.tsx, new components/editorial/* and components/ads/*.

Epic 2 · Public

Homepage, city and article templates

Replace the flat home lead with the existing bento/Three architecture, redesign city editions, add article briefing/sources/rail/recirculation and keep all core content server-rendered.

Primary files: app/(frontend)/page.tsx, app/(frontend)/[city]/page.tsx, app/(frontend)/[city]/[idSlug]/page.tsx, HeroBentoGrid.tsx, three/Ocean*.tsx.

Epic 3 · Yield

Placement policy and event chain

Make placements Payload-driven; request near view; record opportunity → decision → render → viewable → click; isolate personalization from cached editorial; add experiment assignment and a safe house-ad fallback.

Primary files: components/AdSlot.tsx replacement, lib/ads.ts, app/api/ads/*, new decision service and aggregation job.

Epic 4 · Flow

Draft-transfer hardening and branded Payload dashboard

Add Hunt’s Pointe preflight/receipt/status, versioned idempotent ingestion, media/provenance mapping and role-aware dashboard components. Preserve Payload conventions so upgrades remain maintainable.

Hunt’s Pointe: DistributePanel.tsx, new SignalDeskPreflight.tsx, supabase/functions/push-signaldesk/index.ts. SignalDesk: integration route, collections, admin components/import map.

Epic 5 · Launch

Build Loop, experiment and staged rollout

Run deterministic tests, visual QA and staging verification. Launch the design to 10% without the density experiment, then 50/50 standard vs revenue placement policy after two clean days. Promote only when viewability and net revenue improve inside guardrails.

09 · Mandatory Build Loop gate

“Looks finished” is not a release state.

Per-epic loop

  1. Spec: name the viewability mechanism, affected routes, explicit exclusions and rollback threshold.
  2. RED: write failing tests for deterministic behavior: draft enforcement, idempotency, access, placement eligibility, consent and event signing.
  3. GREEN: implement the smallest change that passes.
  4. Visual run: render affected pages at agreed viewports, states and themes; compare screenshots.
  5. Interaction run: keyboard, touch, reduced motion, consent withdrawal, offline/error and low-demand fallbacks.
  6. Revenue run: staging ad requests, opportunity/decision/render/viewable chain, no duplicate impression, reconciliation sample.
  7. Evidence: attach command output, screenshots, trace IDs and monitoring links to the PR. No evidence means the gate is open.

Visual and device matrix

  • 390×844 iPhone-class layout: light/dark, standard/revenue, anchor open/dismissed.
  • 412×915 Android-class layout: reduced motion, Save-Data/static poster and keyboard-equivalent switch access.
  • 768×1024 tablet portrait and landscape.
  • 1440×900 and 1920×1080 desktop with and without the right rail.
  • Chrome, Safari and Firefox current; VoiceOver and NVDA spot checks.
  • No WebGL, no ad fill, slow creative, rejected creative, expired campaign and consent-withdrawal states.
  • Draft transfer success, timeout, duplicate retry, invalid signature, rights-review and unknown-taxonomy states.

Experiment design

Randomize by anonymous session, 50/50: standard placement policy vs revenue placement policy. Hold demand sources, floors and creative mix constant so the test isolates placement. Run at least two complete weekly cycles and until a power analysis reaches 80% power for an 8% net-revenue lift. Primary mechanism metric: viewability. Business outcome: net revenue per 1,000 sessions. Guardrails: LCP, INP, CLS, pages/session, bounce, 7-day return, correction rate and ad complaints.

10 · Deliberately deferred

Scope removed because it does not unlock the next dollar safely.

  • Automatic publishing from Hunt’s Pointe: violates the editorial and governance boundary.
  • CPM-driven topic selection: AI may recommend formats from aggregated signals, never coverage solely for estimated price.
  • Prebid Server or a full exchange: premature before private direct/PMP inventory, event integrity and viewability are working.
  • Timed ad refresh: deferred until viewability, demand permission and advertiser trust are measurable.
  • Infinite scroll: obscures page boundaries, consent context and session measurement; strong contextual recirculation comes first.
  • Autoplay video and audio: harms trust, data use and performance.
  • Full 3D article pages: atmosphere belongs at the brand entrance; evidence pages prioritize text, photography, maps and charts.
  • Replacing Payload: the generated admin is an asset. Brand and extend it instead of creating a parallel CMS.
  • Individualized editorial ranking: launch with explicit city/topic follows and contextual relevance; do not mix private ad state into cached editorial HTML.
Final approval still required:

The prototype retains “Hampton Roads” and treats History as a vertical. Before production design begins, approve the final publication name, legal wordmark and whether “America begins at the water” is a launch campaign line or permanent tagline.
